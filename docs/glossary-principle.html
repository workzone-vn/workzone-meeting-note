How glossary.yaml improves transcript quality

Vietnamese meetings are full of English terms — spoken with Vietnamese phonetics. Speech recognition writes down what it hears, not what was meant. The glossary closes that gap at the writing stage, without ever touching the recognizer or the raw recording.

The failure mode

Whisper transcribes Vietnamese-accented English phonetically. The words below are what a perfectly good model actually hears:

đét laishould bedeadline ây pi aishould beAPI phít báchshould befeedback mít tinhshould bemeeting rô đờ mépshould beroadmap cờ rimshould beCRM

Design principles

Fix at writing time, not recognition time

No fine-tuning, no custom acoustic model. The recognizer stays stock and upgradeable; quality improves in the step where context is richest.

Hints, not rules

Entries are guidance the writer applies with judgment. “sêu” becomes “sale” only where the sentence is actually about sales.

The raw transcript is sacred

Every correction is additive. The verbatim record survives untouched, so nothing is ever silently rewritten or lost.

Private data stays private

The product tier contains zero personal data. The personal tier lives only in ~/wz-bien-ban/ and reaches no one but your own Claude account.
